I'm Daniel Vykoukal, a master's student in Economic Research at [CERGE-EI](https://www.cerge-ei.cz/) in Prague. I completed my bachelor's in Economics at the University of Economics in Prague (VŠE) in 2025.

My interests sit at the intersection of macroeconomics, monetary policy, and applied econometrics — in particular time-series and Bayesian methods (VAR/BVAR), and the way macroeconomic shocks shape expectations and outcomes across heterogeneous agents.

What I'm working on
======
- **Research Assistant** to Stephanie Ettmeier at Charles University, on *Distributional Effects of Macroeconomic Shocks on Expectations*. My work involves large-scale data cleaning and exploratory analysis in Python, building reproducible workflows, and translating empirical results for both technical and non-technical audiences.
- **Coursework** at CERGE-EI in Applied Macroeconomics, Applied Microeconomics, and Statistics.

Tools I work with
======
Python, R, MATLAB, EViews, LaTeX. Methods: OLS, time-series analysis, VAR/BVAR, panel data.
